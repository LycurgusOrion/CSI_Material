About CSI

Computer Society of India (CSI) is the first and largest body of computer professionals in India. It was started on 6 March 1965 by a few computer professionals and has now grown to be the national body representing computer professionals. Today the CSI takes pride in being the largest and most professionally managed association of and for IT professionals in India. Being closely associated with Students the Society has developed a well-established net-work of "Student Branches" all across the country. CSI has 488 student branches with more than 90,000 Student Volunteers across the nation. CSI regularly organizes –

  • SEARCC Programming Contest
  • Alan Turning Quiz
  • Discover Thinking Project Contest
  • On-line Programming Contest

    • In addition, CSI also encourages the students with the following benefits:

  • CSI minor research funding up to 50,000 INR
  • Discounted registration fee for CSI conventions & seminars
  • Opportunity to publish in CSI Communications and CSI Adhyayan
  • Participation of joint events of CSI with its MOU partner/Organization
  • Concessional rates for CSI Conferences and Tutorials For more information, visit the CSI website at http://www.csi-india.org/

    • Membership

    The NorthCap University is an Educational institution member of Computer Society of India (CSI) with membership number I02176 and has an active student chapter.
    Any graduate/post graduate student can become a member of the student chapter. To extend the opportunity to be a student member of CSI, the registration window will be opened every year from 1st to 20th of August. The membership fee for one year is 350 INR. Interested students can contact Dr. A. Charan Kumari, Associate Professor, Department of CSE & IT (R.No. 130 or charankumari@ncuindia.edu). Each student will be issued with a CSI membership identity card from the CSI Head Quarters, Chennai.

    Achievements

  • The CSI Student Chapter of Manav Rachna International University organized an Inter-College Technical Fest on 28th February 2017. Three first year CSE students – Aaditya Verma, Abhishek jain and Ravi Rathee participated in various competitions. The team of three secured first position in the ‘Technical Quiz’ and Aaditya Verma bagged the second prize in ‘Programming Logic’.

  • The students have developed amazing 2D games including Mario, Angry Birds, Color switch etc. and 3D games including multi-player games

  • The students have designed and implemented around 25 small applications in IoT, including security gadget for homes.

  • 9 students got an opportunity to do internship at Open Hardware Days (OHD) at Hyderabad.
